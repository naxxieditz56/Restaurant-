Our Story

From 1933 to today, a Louisville tradition

Through the Years

1933

Founding Years

Jack Fry and his wife Flossie open a sportsman's hangout, known for gambling, amateur boxing, and horse racing.

1972

Temporary Closure

The restaurant closes and the space is leased to "Por Que No," a Mexican restaurant.

1981

Reopening

Joseph Mees, Sandra Antle Pike, and Sara German Peters reopen with Jack Fry's blessing.

1985

Transformation

Susan Seiller purchases and transforms Jack Fry's from a casual tavern into a fine-dining institution.

2008

New Stewardship

Stephanie J. Meeks, who started as an employee in 1996, purchases the restaurant.

Today

Continuing Legacy

Preserving historic charm while offering contemporary Southern fine dining.
